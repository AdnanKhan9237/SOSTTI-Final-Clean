Discipline Policy

Maintaining a respectful, safe, and productive learning environment

Policy Statement

SOS Technical Training Institute is committed to maintaining a disciplined environment that promotes respect, safety, and academic excellence. This discipline policy establishes clear expectations for behavior and outlines fair, consistent procedures for addressing disciplinary issues while focusing on corrective rather than punitive measures where appropriate.

Code of Conduct

Expected Behavior

  • Respect for all individuals regardless of background
  • Punctuality and regular attendance
  • Proper use of institute property and facilities
  • Academic honesty and integrity
  • Appropriate language and professional demeanor
  • Compliance with safety regulations

Categories of Offenses

Category Examples Typical Consequences
Minor Offenses Late arrival, improper uniform, class disruption Verbal warning, written warning, parent notification
Moderate Offenses Repeated minor offenses, unauthorized absence, disrespect Written warning, detention, community service
Serious Offenses Fighting, theft, vandalism, academic dishonesty Suspension, fine, behavioral contract
Gross Misconduct Violence, harassment, drug/alcohol possession Expulsion, police involvement, permanent record

Zero Tolerance Offenses

The institute maintains a zero-tolerance policy for the following offenses, which will result in immediate suspension and potential expulsion:

  • Physical violence or threats of violence
  • Possession of weapons or dangerous items
  • Sexual harassment or assault
  • Drug or alcohol possession or use on campus
  • Gross insubordination or willful destruction of property

Disciplinary Procedures

Step 1: Incident Reporting

  • Immediate reporting of disciplinary incidents
  • Written incident report by staff member
  • Preliminary fact-finding by department head
  • Interim measures if safety concerns exist

Step 2: Investigation

  • Fair and impartial investigation
  • Opportunity for student/staff to present their version
  • Collection of evidence and witness statements
  • Timely completion of investigation

Step 3: Decision & Action

  • Appropriate disciplinary action based on offense severity
  • Written notification of decision
  • Clear explanation of consequences
  • Implementation of corrective measures

Step 4: Follow-up & Monitoring

  • Regular monitoring of behavioral improvement
  • Support services for rehabilitation
  • Documentation of progress
  • Closure of case upon satisfactory improvement

Disciplinary Actions

Action Description Applicable For
Verbal Warning Informal discussion about behavior First-time minor offenses
Written Warning Formal written record of offense Repeated minor or first moderate offenses
Detention Additional time spent on campus Moderate behavioral issues
Community Service Service to institute community Moderate offenses requiring restitution
Suspension Temporary removal from institute Serious or repeated offenses
Expulsion Permanent removal from institute Gross misconduct or zero-tolerance offenses

Appeals Process

  • Right to appeal disciplinary decisions
  • Written appeal submitted within 5 working days
  • Appeals committee comprising senior staff members
  • Fair hearing with representation allowed
  • Final decision communicated within 10 working days
  • External mediation available for expulsion cases

Restorative Practices

The institute emphasizes restorative approaches to discipline that focus on repairing harm and rebuilding relationships:

  • Mediation sessions between affected parties
  • Restitution for damages or losses
  • Behavioral contracts with clear improvement goals
  • Counseling and support services
  • Mentorship programs for at-risk students

Staff Disciplinary Procedures

  • Separate disciplinary procedures for staff members
  • Progressive discipline approach
  • HR department involvement in staff disciplinary matters
  • Right to representation during disciplinary proceedings
  • Compliance with labor laws and employment contracts

Record Keeping

  • Confidential disciplinary records maintained
  • Records retained for specified periods based on offense severity
  • Access restricted to authorized personnel
  • Expungement of minor offense records after improvement period
  • Statistical analysis for pattern identification

Policy Review

This discipline policy is reviewed annually by the Disciplinary Committee to ensure it remains effective, fair, and aligned with best practices in educational discipline.
