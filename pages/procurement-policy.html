Procurement Policy

Transparent, efficient, and cost-effective acquisition of goods and services

Policy Statement

SOS Technical Training Institute is committed to procuring goods and services in a manner that ensures transparency, fairness, competition, and value for money. This policy establishes the framework for all procurement activities to support the institute's educational mission while maintaining fiscal responsibility.

Procurement Principles

  • Transparency: All procurement processes are open and documented
  • Fairness: Equal opportunity for all qualified suppliers
  • Competition: Competitive bidding for better value
  • Accountability: Clear responsibility for procurement decisions
  • Value for Money: Optimal balance of cost, quality, and sustainability
  • Ethical Standards: Adherence to highest ethical standards

Procurement Thresholds & Methods

Low Value Purchases (Up to Rs. 25,000)

  • Direct purchase from registered vendors
  • Minimum two verbal quotations
  • Department head approval required

Medium Value Purchases (Rs. 25,001 - Rs. 200,000)

  • Written quotations from at least three suppliers
  • Purchase committee evaluation
  • Director approval required

High Value Purchases (Above Rs. 200,000)

  • Formal tender process
  • Public advertisement in newspapers
  • Tender committee evaluation
  • Managing committee approval required

Procurement Process

Stage Activities Timeline
Needs Assessment Requirement identification and budget approval As needed
Vendor Selection Quotation/tender process and evaluation 2-4 weeks
Purchase Order Contract preparation and approval 1 week
Delivery & Inspection Goods receipt and quality verification Upon delivery
Payment Processing Document verification and payment 2-3 weeks

Vendor Management

  • Maintain registered vendor database
  • Regular performance evaluation of vendors
  • Blacklisting procedure for non-performing suppliers
  • Encourage local and small business participation
  • Preference for environmentally responsible suppliers

Emergency Procurement

  • Emergency situations allow expedited procurement
  • Post-facto approval from appropriate authority
  • Documentation maintained for audit purposes
  • Limited to genuine emergency situations only
  • Report to procurement committee for review

Conflict of Interest

  • Staff must declare any conflict of interest
  • Exclusion from procurement decisions where conflict exists
  • No acceptance of gifts or favors from suppliers
  • Annual conflict of interest declarations
  • Strict disciplinary action for policy violations

Documentation & Record Keeping

  • Complete procurement files maintained for 7 years
  • Digital records backed up regularly
  • Access restricted to authorized personnel
  • Regular internal audits of procurement processes
  • Transparent reporting to stakeholders

Sustainability Considerations

  • Preference for environmentally friendly products
  • Energy-efficient equipment prioritized
  • Local suppliers encouraged to reduce carbon footprint
  • Recyclable and reusable materials preferred
  • Sustainability criteria in tender evaluation

Policy Review

This procurement policy is reviewed annually by the Finance and Procurement Committee to ensure compliance with best practices and regulatory requirements.
